# Branding Notes

## Current Product Direction

The product is not just a notes app, bookmark manager, or insurance tool.

It is closer to:

> An admin operations memory workspace.

It helps small teams remember and search for:

- Daily checklists
- Portal links
- What a link is used for
- Payer/vendor/platform notes
- Issue resolution history
- "How we handle this" procedures
- Team knowledge that would otherwise live in someone's head

## About "IA"

Initial idea:

> IA = Intelligence Assistant / Intelligent Assistant

This has some appeal because the product does assist administrative work. But as a brand name, `IA` may be too abstract.

Concerns:

- It does not immediately explain what the product does.
- It can sound too much like a generic AI assistant.
- It may be confused with Information Architecture, Internal Audit, or Intelligent Automation.
- It may be harder to search, protect, or use as a domain.

Better use:

- Keep the "intelligent assistant" idea as a tagline or positioning phrase.
- Use a warmer, more concrete product name as the brand.

Example:

> Aidebase — intelligent assistance for everyday admin work.

## Naming Directions

### 1. Admin Memory Direction

Names in this direction should communicate recall, memory, and finding operational knowledge quickly.

Options:

- Memospace
- Admina
- Recall Desk
- Desk Memory
- Office Memory
- Workmemo
- Nestwork
- Recallly

Favorite from this group:

> Recall Desk

Why:

- Clear and practical.
- Suggests that the workspace helps staff remember what to do.
- Works well for front desk, admin, and small business operations.

### 2. Assistant Direction

Names in this direction should feel helpful, calm, and slightly smart without becoming too AI-heavy.

Options:

- Ivy Assist
- Mira
- Aidebase
- Clara Desk
- Nora
- Assistly
- Desk Aide

Favorite from this group:

> Aidebase

Why:

- Combines "aide" and "knowledge base."
- Feels more brandable than "Admin Tool."
- Has assistant energy without sounding like a chatbot.

### 3. Workspace / Notion-Like Direction

Names in this direction should feel like a flexible but structured place to keep work organized.

Options:

- Basewell
- Nestbase
- Workwell
- Deskbase
- Opspace
- PocketOps

Favorite from this group:

> Basewell

Why:

- Softer and more brand-like.
- Suggests a well-kept base for daily work.
- Less literal, closer to the kind of name that can grow beyond clinics.

### 4. Admin Operations Direction

Names in this direction are more direct and B2B. They should make the value easier to understand quickly.

Options:

- OpsMemo
- AdminOS
- Frontdesk OS
- OpsNote
- Team Recall
- OfficeOps

Favorite from this group:

> OpsMemo

Why:

- Short and understandable.
- Signals operations + memory.
- Good fit for admin teams, clinics, and small businesses.

## Current Shortlist

Top candidates:

1. Aidebase
2. Recall Desk
3. OpsMemo
4. Basewell
5. Deskwise

## Positioning Thoughts

If the goal is a more brandable, Notion-like name:

- Aidebase
- Basewell

If the goal is clear and practical for small business buyers:

- Recall Desk
- OpsMemo

## Possible Taglines

For Aidebase:

> Intelligent assistance for everyday admin work.

For Recall Desk:

> The memory layer for small business operations.

For OpsMemo:

> Checklists, links, and how-to notes for admin teams.

For Basewell:

> A calmer workspace for the details your team cannot afford to forget.

## Current Preference

The strongest overall candidates are:

- **Aidebase** if the brand should feel polished, flexible, and product-like.
- **Recall Desk** if the brand should immediately explain the practical value.
- **OpsMemo** if the brand should feel direct, B2B, and admin-operations focused.

The "IA" idea is still useful, but likely better as positioning rather than the main product name.
