# Product Strategy Notes

## Core Direction

The current Lake Hills OA app can grow beyond one clinic by becoming an admin operations workspace.

The broader positioning is:

> A simple admin workspace for checklists, links, and "how we handle this" notes.

The product should not try to become another Notion. Its value is being more ready-to-use, more guided, and more focused on daily administrative work.

## Product Category

Working category:

- Admin Operations Workspace
- Administrative Memory Workspace
- Small Business Admin Workspace

Chinese explanation:

> 给行政、前台、office manager 和小团队用的内部工作记忆库。

The app helps teams keep searchable operational knowledge in one place:

- Daily and weekly checklists
- Portal links and what each link is used for
- Payer, vendor, platform, or account directories
- Case notes and issue-resolution history
- "How we handle this" procedures
- Shared team notes that should not live only in one person's memory

## Why This Is More Than Bookmarks

Bookmarks only store a link.

This product stores the context around the link:

- What the link is for
- When to use it
- Which payer, vendor, platform, or workflow it belongs to
- What previous staff learned
- What to do when something goes wrong
- Which checklist or procedure it supports

The real value is not "saving links." The value is reducing repeated questions, forgotten procedures, and handoff loss.

## First Niche

Start with small clinic admin/front-desk teams because Lake Hills is already a strong real template.

Best early users:

- Acupuncture clinics
- Dental offices
- Massage clinics
- Chiropractic clinics
- Physical therapy offices
- Small billing/front-desk teams

These teams often have:

- Many portals and links
- Repeated daily procedures
- Insurance, billing, or payment lookup complexity
- Staff turnover and training needs
- Knowledge scattered across browsers, paper notes, chats, and memory

## Future Templates

Lake Hills OA can become the first template, not the whole product.

Potential templates:

- Small Clinic Admin
- Dental Office Admin
- Massage Studio Admin
- Chiropractic Clinic Admin
- Vendor / Portal Directory
- Office Opening & Closing Checklist
- Claims / Issue Resolution Log
- Job Application Tracker
- Personal Admin Hub
- HR Onboarding Links

## Will Companies Pay?

Some will, but the paid value is not "a place to write things."

The paid value is:

- New staff can find answers without asking the owner every time
- Teams stop losing operational knowledge when someone leaves
- Common links, payer notes, and procedures are searchable
- Fewer mistakes happen from using the wrong portal or forgetting context
- Training and handoff become easier

The strongest sales message:

> New front desk staff can find the right link, payer note, and daily checklist without asking you every time.

Chinese version:

> 新人不用每件事都问老板，常用链接、处理方法、每日流程都能自己搜到。

## Likely Pricing Direction

Early pricing should stay simple:

- Free: one workspace, limited entries or users
- Starter: around $9/month for solo admin use
- Team: around $19-$29/month for small teams
- Setup service: one-time $99-$199 to help a small business organize their checklists, links, and SOP cards

The setup service may be easier to sell at first than pure monthly SaaS, because small businesses often need help organizing their existing messy notes.

## Next Product Step

Recommended next step:

> Build template selection before building full multi-workspace support.

Near-term roadmap:

1. Make category creation template-based.
2. Standardize templates: Checklist, Case/SOP Cards, Link Directory, Blank Cards.
3. Add workspace settings: workspace name, color, business type, default template.
4. Extract Lake Hills as a reusable seed template.
5. Test with 1-3 real small business or clinic admin users.

Important product principle:

> Template first, workspace later.

This keeps the product focused and avoids turning it into an overly broad blank workspace too early.

## Validation Question

Before worrying about a large market, validate this:

> Can 3 small teams put their scattered admin notes, links, checklists, and procedures into the app and keep using it for two weeks?

If yes, the product has a real use case.

If no, the issue is likely template fit, workflow friction, or unclear value positioning.
